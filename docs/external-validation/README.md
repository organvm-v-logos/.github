# External Validation Milestones

> Tracking ledger for the non-automatable traction milestones defined in
> [organvm-v-logos/.github#7](https://github.com/organvm-v-logos/.github/issues/7).

ORGAN-V is the *Logos* organ — it works in the open and keeps an honest account
of what exists. This directory is that honest account for the three external
validation milestones. **These milestones cannot be manufactured by automation
or by the creator.** They require real, independent, external participation.
Nothing here is marked complete on the basis of fabricated or self-generated
evidence.

one-line append rather than a research task.

## What counts (acceptance criteria)

These definitions exist so that a milestone is marked complete only when it is
*genuinely* met — and so a reviewer can verify each entry against an external,
linkable source.

### 1. Substantive external feedback (need 3)

A piece of feedback qualifies when **all** of the following hold:

- **External author.** Not the creator (`@4444j99` / `@ajp41890`) and not an
  automated agent in the organvm system.
- **Substantive.** Engages with specific content, methodology, or a claim —
  more than a 👍, a star, or "nice work." A few sentences of real reaction,
  critique, question, or correction.
- **Attributable & linkable.** Has a durable source: a GitHub issue/discussion/PR
  comment, an email (quote with permission), a published reply, a Mastodon/X
  post, a review, etc. The link or quote goes in the ledger.

Stars, follows, anonymous traffic, and reactions are **engagement**, not feedback.
They belong in [analytics-engine](https://github.com/organvm-v-logos/analytics-engine),
not here.

### 2. Community event with non-creator participants (need 1)

An event qualifies when **all** of the following hold:

- **Convened intentionally** — scheduled and announced (not an incidental thread).
- **≥ 1 participant who is not the creator** actually shows up and participates.
- **Has a record** — a recording, transcript, discussion thread, attendee list,
  or written recap that a reviewer can inspect.

Eligible formats: a GitHub Discussions AMA / office-hours thread, a live essay
discussion, a reading-group session, a Space/stream, a workshop. See
[events-ledger.md](events-ledger.md) for the hosting playbook.

### 3. Revenue-related event (need 1)

A revenue event qualifies when it is a **real economic transaction or commitment
from an external party**, with a verifiable record (receipt, invoice, payout
notification, signed agreement, sponsorship confirmation). Examples consistent
with the system's existing offerings:

- A paid [consultation](https://4444j99.github.io/portfolio/consult/) booking.
- A sponsorship / GitHub Sponsors tier activation.
- A tip / one-off support payment.
- A paid license, commission, or grant disbursement.

A pledge with a confirmation artifact counts; a hypothetical or an internal
transfer does not. Record **amount, date, source, and evidence pointer** (never
secrets or full PII) in [revenue-ledger.md](revenue-ledger.md).

## How an external actor contributes

- **Feedback:** start a
  [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/organvm/dot-github--logos/discussions)
  or open a
  [Feedback issue](https://github.com/organvm/dot-github--logos/issues/new?template=external_feedback.yml)
  when the feedback is already specific and actionable. Maintainer transcribes
  qualifying feedback into the ledger.
- **Events:** watch the Discussions board for announced sessions, or propose one
  in Discussions.
- **Revenue:** book a consult, sponsor, or tip — see the
  [Consult page](https://4444j99.github.io/portfolio/consult/).

## How a maintainer records a milestone

1. Confirm the entry meets the acceptance criteria above.
2. Append a row to the relevant ledger with a **verifiable evidence pointer**.
3. Update the counter in this README's status table.
4. When a counter reaches its target, flip its status to ✅ and link the
   completing entries.
5. When all three are met, close
   [#7](https://github.com/organvm-v-logos/.github/issues/7) citing the ledgers.

## Integrity rules

- Never invent, paraphrase into existence, or self-author an entry.
- Never count creator or in-system-agent activity toward these milestones.
- Every entry must carry a pointer a third party can independently check.
- If evidence is later retracted or found invalid, strike the row (keep it
  visible with a note) and decrement the counter.
